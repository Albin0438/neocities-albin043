Why I Prefer Linux Even Though Many Still Stick With Windows

I recently bought my first laptop. Before that, I didn’t own one. Using only my smartphone, I spent a lot of time watching and listening to many YouTube channels and podcasts. Because of this, I became a big fan of open-source software and started to understand the importance of privacy, even though many people say we have nothing to hide.

My new laptop came with Windows 11 pre-installed. Even though my laptop has 16GB RAM and a 512GB SSD, Windows slowly started to annoy me. I noticed that only about 260GB of storage was free on my disk. At first I thought the shop had scammed me, but after a few days I realized it was not a problem with my laptop. It was simply Windows.

After discovering this, I decided not to continue using it. Without a second thought I cleared my entire disk and installed Linux. I was impressed. Windows showed around 260GB of free storage, but on Linux I had about 502GB available and a much faster system.

Choosing Linux was not easy at first because there are many distributions. Each has its own advantages and disadvantages. After trying Debian, Fedora (Workstation, KDE Plasma, Cinnamon), and Linux Mint, I finally stayed with Mint. It felt perfect for me as a beginner because it is simple, user-friendly, and most tools are already installed.

Booting into Linux Mint honestly felt like going from hell to heaven. Windows had constant updates, privacy concerns, and slow update times. Linux felt refreshing and lightweight in comparison.

In practice, I found alternatives for almost every service available on Windows. The main reason many people still stay with Windows is gaming, but that is not important for me. I am more interested in privacy, web development, and programming.

Now I am completely comfortable with my Linux setup and enjoy using it more every day. Linux Mint has been a great experience for me.
