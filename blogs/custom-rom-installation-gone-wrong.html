When a Custom ROM Installation Went Wrong

Apr 23, 2026 · 5 min read

Today I decided to flash another custom ROM on my Redmi Note 8. I was already using InfinityX, a LineageOS-based ROM, but I had installed the version bundled with Google apps.

Over time, I started noticing how much background activity was tied to Google services. That made me uncomfortable. I wanted something more privacy-focused, so I decided to switch to a cleaner LineageOS build.

But I made a careless mistake—I downloaded the wrong ROM file. Instead of the Redmi Note 8 build, I grabbed one meant for another device. I didn’t double-check, assuming I had done this enough times for it to “just work.”

It didn’t.

My phone went into a bootloop. I couldn’t even flash a recovery through fastboot. And since that phone was my only way to access mobile data, I was suddenly cut off from the internet.

What made it worse was realizing I had faced a similar issue before—but instead of properly learning the steps or writing them down, I had relied on quick fixes and moved on. This time, that shortcut came back to bite me.

For a while, I was stuck. No internet, no quick solution. I had to wait until I could access another connection.

But something unexpected happened.

Without my phone, I stepped outside more. I spent time in the garden, noticed things I usually ignored, and actually disconnected. Ironically, the thing I had been trying to “fix” with apps—screen time—was solved instantly when the device itself was gone.

I even went back to watching full-length content I had downloaded earlier instead of short-form distractions. It felt… different. Better.

Now my concern is simple: once I fix the phone, will I fall back into the same habits?

Maybe the real issue isn’t the software or the ROM. Maybe it’s the relationship we have with the device itself.
