Please Delete your Whatsapp ASAP.

May 20, 2026 · 4 min read

Dear users,

Don't use WhatsApp if you can.

Seriously, don't use it.

We all know WhatsApp is the most popular messaging app owned by Meta, Inc. They just successfully acquired WhatsApp from Jan Koum and Brian Acton.

Once, WhatsApp was a perfect messenger, a simple and efficient communication method. But after Mark Zuckerberg cast his evil eye on it, WhatsApp is no longer an efficient messenger; it's now an efficient surveillance tool.

Bloated by design.

They claim these are features and make users' lives productive, but as far as I know, these are just bloat. These are designed by many psychologists and engineers to hook users on the app and make them spend their precious time infinitely without realizing it was a waste of time.

Another intelligent psychological trick they are using is that the more users they have/the more users depend on WhatsApp, the less chance they have of getting caught or punished. For example, humans are social beings. Also, humans like to follow others like sheep. If 10 people are using WhatsApp as their communication platform and the 11th person refuses to use it, he/she gets isolated. So in fear of isolation or getting out from their gang, someone who knows the disadvantages of WhatsApp and actively refuses to use WhatsApp will still use it because they have no choice. Everyone is on it.

Garbage things in WhatsApp:

  • Meta AI: a built-in intelligent spy.
  • Status: Keep tracking daily updates of their lives.
  • Communities: unnecessary stuff. The group was almost perfect.
  • Ads: part-time spying to make money. (Anyway, they spy 24×7).
  • Premium features: added garbage features to extract users' money.

Dangerous features that affect users' mental health:

  • Reading recipients' questions challenges our self-respect and makes us worry about others' perspectives on us.
  • Status: Daily comparison.
  • Notifications: triggers an instant need to reply. Catches attention.
  • Meta accounts: Sync your profiles on multiple platforms and help them create a perfect profile about you.
  • Ad preferences: Choose what types of ads to see. But can we say no to ads? No.

Is Meta the only company doing it?

The short answer is no. But Meta is the most irresponsible data harvesting surveillance company I have ever seen in my life. They own a portion of the internet. Mostly used apps like Instagram, WhatsApp, Facebook, Threads, and Meta.ai all come under Meta Inc. Still, they are not serious about user privacy or security at all. The recent decision to introduce ads to a huge messaging platform like WhatsApp is just dumb. The decision to remove E2E from Instagram is dumb. Creating profiles (shadow profiles) of users who don't want to participate in any of these is also creepy. How can such a company be this creepy? And the disturbing thing is that even though there are alternatives, people still use this crap and actively talk about privacy being lost and surveillance being everywhere.

I believe the first step of stopping all of this stuff happening is to do what needs to be done. If you really care about 'privacy,' you must stop using privacy-invading services instead of saying, 'Oh, they're harvesting my data, and I can't do anything about it.' Yes, I know; as I said, if we went in a different path, we would lose our gang/group of people. But that's absolutely ok until you lose yourself. The most important person you have to keep in your life and satisfy is yourself, not anyone else.

Actually, what's the point of a life living a life for satisfying someone else instead of doing what we really want to do? What's the point of a life following others instead of doing something different? Why do we always need someone to guide us? Why do we always talk and interact with them when they don't even value or hear us? Stop. Please stop living like a prisoner.

There are privacy-respecting and freedom-giving platforms that exist. For example:

  • Signal
  • Telegram (unencrypted)
  • Matrix
  • Session

Everything has its own disadvantages, but nothing is as creepy as WhatsApp or Meta.
