The Illusion of Connection

How social media masks our struggles and traps us in a cycle of consumption.

Social media has become an important part of modern life, giving us an illusion of meaning within the system. Even if we create or not, the level of consumption is extremely high. It allows individuals to curate their lives and present a version of reality that can be far from what is real.

This digital era creates a sense of connection and purpose, yet it often masks the underlying struggles and discontent that many face. People find themselves scrolling through feeds filled with carefully crafted images and stories, leading them to believe that happiness and fulfillment are just a post away.

In this way, social media becomes not connection — but distraction.

It diverts attention from the harsher realities of life and the existential questions that linger beneath the surface.

In this context, many individuals find themselves trapped in a cycle of work and consumption, laboring for companies and organizations that prioritize profit over people. The relentless pursuit of money becomes the meaning of living — survival.

Yet it often leads to a feeling of emptiness and disillusionment. People work tirelessly in roles that do not bring them any joy or satisfaction. It is only for affording basic necessities of life.

Existence begins to feel like a form of modern slavery, where individuals are bound by the expectations of society, all while chasing an elusive dream of financial security and happiness.

The question continues: why live such a life? Why continue to endure this cycle?

The reality is that many feel powerless to break free from the constraints imposed by a system that values profit over well-being. The fear of financial instability and the societal pressure to conform — to have a “good job” and be financially secure — can be overwhelming.

Life becomes less about living, and more about surviving within expectations.

However, it is important to understand that life holds more than wealth. Happiness, as often presented, can feel like an illusion — a reward mechanism shaped by those in power.

By questioning the status quo and seeking deeper meaning beyond material gain, individuals can begin to reclaim their lives and find fulfillment in ways that are not dictated by corporate or societal demands.

Ultimately, the journey toward a more meaningful existence requires courage — and a willingness to challenge the narratives that have been imposed upon us.
